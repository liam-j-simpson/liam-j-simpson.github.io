Identity, Values and Strengths

The values that are the most important to me are values of optimism, making the most of ones time, working hard and socialising with others.

  1. Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

    I had a client request some content from a video production that framed them in a light that was untrue. It was a difficult request to receive, on one side I wanted to go above and beyond for my client by providing them with what they were after but on the other side I couldn’t give them content that was going to portray a message that was untrue. Truth and integrity is a big value for me so I explained to the client that I wouldn’t supply the content and gave them the reason. Reflecting on this decision I honestly think the client was a bit shocked by the pushback but I think it was the right decision to make at the time and I’m glad I stuck to my values.

  2. Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

    I was raised in New Zealand with a mum and dad, both NZ / European. They raised me and my two sisters to value exploring NZ’s natural landscape through frequent camping trips and the occasional hike together. This value for New Zealand’s natural landscape is what continues to be a large part of my identity. Kiwi culture can come with some other influences and cultural norms. It’s common for kiwis to steer clear of discussing their feelings with each other and this is something that I definitely subscribed to in the past. The effects of this is something that I became aware of in my 20’s through seeing people’s struggles with mental health. It took witnessing these detrimental effects for me to re-evaluate my identity and what areas of ‘culture’ I am influenced by. I’ve worked in the creative industry for six years now as a photographer and have been influenced by creatives of different disciplines around me. This has led me to have an appreciation and strong value for architecture, design and just generally the beauty of everyday things. Through being around a group of friends with good emotional intelligence I’ve taken an interest in mental health and in particular the healthy use of technology. Social media use is one of my favourite topics and I’m intrigued to see whether social media can become truly social and built for the benefit of the customer.

  3. Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

    My top five strengths based on the Clifton strength finders are: activator, empathy, futuristic, strategic and woo. My strengths in empathy and woo will support me in the human skills section of the course. I really enjoy relating to others and find that I work with people well. My activator strength is great for turning ideas into action and strategic means that I like to look at things from different perspectives before choosing a unique path to a solution, this will be quite usefull for problem solving.

  4. Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

    Being an activator isn’t always helpful for things like coding! I love to dive straight into solving problems with less thought for planning and this could be an issue when it comes to problem solving with coding. I’ll need to make sure I plan effectively before getting into the nitty gritty.

  5. Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

    I had a video shoot that I was directing where the output was films about environmental sustainability. The production team I was working with had some tension at the time and I needed to get them onboard with the research and scriptwriting I had done. My strategy for overcoming the tension was making sure that each individual on the team felt empowered within their role and had clear instruction on what they needed to achieve to make the production successful. Throughout the project I checked in with each team member to make sure they were happy with the process. If I was to do this again I would discuss the tension directly with the team. In the past I’ve had a less direct approach, making each team member feel valued individually rather than consulting the issues of the team upfront with the group.

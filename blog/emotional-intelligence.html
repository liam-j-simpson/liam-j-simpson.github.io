Emotional Intelligence

What is Emotional Intelligence?

Emotional Intelligence is the ability to healthily navigate interpersonal relationships using emotions. There’s four sub skills within the over arching topic of Emotional Intelligence, these are:

  1. Perceiving emotions: Accurately reading verbal and non verbal queues to understand your own or other’s emotions.
  2. Reasoning with emotions: Allowing your emotions to stimulate cognitive thoughts.
  3. Understanding emotions: Searching for understanding of these emotions. E.g. what’s the source of this persons anger? Are they angry at me or is these something external to our relationship that has caused this anger?
  4. Managing emotions: Controlling how we healthily respond to our emotions or the emotions of others.

How is EQ different to IQ?

EQ is the ‘soft skills’ intelligence that we carry. EQ is commonly shown by how well we work with people. IQ is the more generic intelligence or ‘hard skills’. IQ is commonly shown through how proficient we are at learning new concepts, new skills, recalling information and solving problems.

Why is EQ important?

Working in a group allows us to achieve more than we ever could as an individual, that’s why organisations exist. However, working in a group is complex. Humans aren’t as black and white as code on a screen. Each person brings a myriad of emotions, values and beliefs to a team and to navigate these complexities is a skill. This skill is what we call EQ.

EQ is needed for a group to understand each other’s emotions and to relate to each other. Without EQ, team’s can’t reach their full potential.

A common issue in the workforce is that employees can have amazing IQ however, their EQ is lacking. EQ is critical to working within a team and therefore, is often of higher priority than IQ in the workplace. As the saying goes, “you can train skills but you can’t train personality.”

Sprint 2 - Technical

Relative, Absolute & Fixed Positioning explained.

6 September 2024

Relative, Absolute and Fixed positioning gives programmers the flexibility to place elements exactly where they'd like them on the page. I'm going to explain these positining systems using the analogy of a carpark.


Relative Positioning

Relative positioning allows the position of an element to be changed from it's origin while still holding space at its origin point. It's as if a car was parked in it's designated carpark but now we've moved that car three parks to the right. No one is going to park in the designated carpark and therefore, that space remains empty. Refer to the code below for the green square's relative position.

Absolute Positioning

Absolute positioning allows the element to be placed exactly where specified, regardless of other elements on the page. This is like a car that decides to park wherever it wants, regardless of the rules. It doesn't have to fit within the painted lines, it just goes where it wants. Fixed Positioning

Fixed positioning defines the position of an element relative to the viewport. Say we're flying a drone above the carpark and our car is positioned in the top left corner of our viewport. If we now move our drone to looking at a different carpark the car with fixed positioning will move with us and will stay fixed in the top left corner of our viewport.
